What is IOT?

IOT (or Internet of Things) generally and collectively refers to all things that are powered and enabled by a network on which they connect and exchange data to. This means that the concept of IOT is implemented on different fieds in our lives. These would include:

How do these work?

Although said object may not be considered as "computing device", they could still exchange data and other information as they are enebled either by the internet, Bluetooth or even near-field communications or NFC through embedded sensors.

Who receives the data?

According to IIOT World, the person or entity who holds ownership to the IOT device, in the sense that they were the ones who patented and manufactured it, would receive the data from these devices and the "data subjects" which are the users of these devices like us.

Who owns the data collected?

Besed on laws implemented to protect the users' privacy rights, it is stated that the customers or data subjects will always have the rights to the information collected by IOT devices. Now, it should be noted that because of this, we should all be aware that as consumers, it is our right to be informed on how data from us is collected and how this is used by those who receive it.
